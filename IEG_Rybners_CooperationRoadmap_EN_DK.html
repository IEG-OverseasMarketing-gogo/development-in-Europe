Section 02 · Solution Intersection

Løsningsskæringspunkter — Hvad vi bygger sammen

Three Areas Where Our Solutions
Become One

Tre områder hvor vores løsninger smelter sammen

The most durable partnerships are built on structural complementarity — not goodwill alone. Here is where IEG and Rybners create something neither could produce independently.

🏭
Solution 01 · Løsning 01

The Climate-Smart Factory Learning Environment

Det klimasmarte fabrikslæringsmiljø

IEG's physical training infrastructure — PLC systems, energy monitoring rigs, digital twin platforms — becomes the material embodiment of Rybners' climate curriculum. Students don't study energy efficiency in a classroom; they engineer it on a live factory floor.

The result is a new category of training environment: one that teaches smart manufacturing through the lens of sustainability — a combination that exists nowhere else at this fidelity.

📜
Solution 02 · Løsning 02

A Jointly-Certified Green Manufacturing Qualification

En fælles certificeret grøn produktionskvalifikation

Korea's NCS competency standards and Denmark's EUX/HTX vocational framework are structurally compatible. A joint certification pathway — co-designed by IEG and Rybners, facilitated by ICDK — would create the first bilaterally recognised Green Smart Manufacturing credential.

A qualification that carries weight in Seoul, Copenhagen, and — critically — in Nairobi, Hanoi, and Manila, where this expertise is most urgently needed.

🌍
Solution 03 · Løsning 03

A Korea–Denmark Consortium for Global Green TVET

Et koreansk-dansk konsortium for global grøn erhvervsuddannelse

The developing world is industrialising rapidly — and doing so with a window to build green from the ground up. Neither IEG alone nor Rybners alone is the complete answer. Together — IEG's hardware, curriculum depth, and ODA channels combined with Rybners' climate education authority and the ICDK's diplomatic reach — the consortium becomes the definitive global partner for climate-aligned industrial workforce development.

Section 03 · Long-Term Outcomes

Langsigtede resultater — Fra vision til virkelighed

What Long-Term Cooperation
Produces

Hvad langsigtet samarbejde producerer

The value of this partnership compounds over time. Each stage builds on the last — deepening the integration, expanding the reach, and ultimately establishing Korea–Denmark as the reference model for green industrial education worldwide.

Year 1 · 2026–2027 · 1. år

Proof of Concept: The Green Smart Factory Classroom

Konceptbevis: Det grønne smarte fabriksklasseværelse

IEG equipment is integrated into Rybners' curriculum for the first time. Students measure real energy consumption, optimise factory layouts through digital twins, and earn credit toward a jointly-developed qualification. What emerges is the first prototype of a learning environment that seamlessly unites Denmark's climate education leadership with Korea's smart manufacturing depth — a combination that does not yet exist anywhere in the world.

IEG Equipment at Rybners Joint Curriculum Design First Certified Cohort MOU / LOI Signed

Year 2 · 2027–2028 · 2. år

Institutionalisation: The Korea–Denmark Green Manufacturing Standard

Institutionalisering: Den koreansk-danske grønne produktionsstandard

The pilot curriculum becomes a formal, bilaterally-accredited programme. Korea's NCS framework and Denmark's EUX/HTX standards are mapped into a single qualification architecture — with ICDK facilitating the inter-governmental recognition agreement. IEG and Rybners co-publish the world's first Green Smart Factory TVET framework, positioning the partnership as a standard-setter rather than a course provider.

Bilateral Accreditation NCS ↔ EUX/HTX Alignment Published TVET Framework ICDK Policy Channel Active

Year 3–4 · 2028–2029 · 3.–4. år

Global Deployment: Exporting the Model to Developing Nations

Global udrulning: Eksport af modellen til udviklingslandene

The validated Korea–Denmark framework is deployed as a turnkey package — IEG's hardware, the joint curriculum, and Rybners' climate education brand — into vocational training centres in Africa and Southeast Asia. Korea's EDCF and KOICA ODA financing channels, combined with Denmark's Danida development cooperation funds, provide a blended public finance mechanism that no single country could unlock alone. The consortium bids together. The consortium wins together.

Africa · SE Asia Deployment KOICA + EDCF Financing Danida Linkage Green Factory Centres Established

Year 5+ · 2030 onwards · 5. år og frem

Legacy: The Reference Model for Climate-Aligned Industrial Education

Arv: Referencemodellen for klimatilpasset industriel uddannelse

By 2030, the IEG–Rybners partnership is no longer a bilateral cooperation — it is a recognised international standard. Governments, international development organisations (ADB, ILO, UNESCO-UNEVOC), and multinational manufacturers look to the Korea–Denmark model when designing climate-responsible workforce development programmes. The partnership has created something neither country could have claimed alone: global authorship over the future of green industrial education.

UNESCO-UNEVOC Recognition ADB / ILO Partnership Global Standard Status Korea–Denmark Reference Model
